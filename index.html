MASCULINISM

The Advocacy of Men's Rights
Definition

The advocacy of men's rights — grounded in equal rights, equal responsibilities, equal opportunities and equal protections under the law.

Our mission is to promote equal rights, responsibilities, opportunities and protections under the law, while addressing issues that uniquely or disproportionately affect men. Together we build a fairer, more just world for all.

Read the Manifesto Core Principles

Reclaiming Masculinism

The movement holds that the term “Masculinism” has been widely mischaracterized and associated with extremist views that do not represent its principles. It seeks to reclaim the term and define it according to its original purpose: the advocacy of men's rights, grounded in equal rights, equal responsibilities, equal opportunities, and equal protections under the law.

Who We Represent

Masculinism is a movement for humans that are biologically XY and identify as heterosexual men. It exists to advocate for their rights, interests, and equal treatment under the law.

The movement welcomes support from anyone who shares its principles, but its primary focus is on addressing issues that uniquely or disproportionately affect this constituency.

Values

  • Provide for and protect, with particular care for the vulnerable, including children and mothers.
  • Defend the family unit and support the right of mothers who choose to be homemakers and educators of their children to do so without discrimination.
  • The movement has no religious affiliation and is secular (laic) at its core.

Research & Health Priority

  • Investigate the reported decline in male fertility and testosterone levels, and support research into their causes and evidence-based ways to protect and improve men's reproductive health and overall well-being.

The Manifesto — a continuation of our principles, values and goals
7

Equality Before the Law

  • No law, policy, or social expectation should discriminate against anyone based solely on sex.
  • Equal standards. Equal treatment. Equal accountability.
  • We oppose all forms of legal bias for or against any sex.
8

Family and Parenting

  • Support the family as the fundamental building block of society.
  • Shared parenting as the legal presumption.
  • Equal parental rights and responsibilities.
  • Protect children's right to frequent and meaningful relationships with both parents.
  • Oppose parental alienation and unjust custody decisions.
  • Support the right of mothers who choose to be homemakers and educators without discrimination or social penalty.
9

Criminal Justice Reform

  • Ensure equal protection for male victims of violence, abuse and exploitation.
  • Gender-neutral laws that recognize all victims.
  • Equal sentencing for equal crimes.
  • Recognize emotional abuse and coercive control against men.
  • Penalties for knowingly false accusations proportionate to the harm caused.
10

Education and Boys

  • Address the educational challenges boys face through early intervention and support.
  • Promote mentoring, literacy, and numeracy programs.
  • Encourage male role models and teachers.
  • Expand access to vocational, technical and practical education.
  • Education policy must respond to need, not ideology.
11

Men's Health Priority

  • Investigate and address the decline in male fertility and testosterone.
  • Increase research into causes, prevention and treatment.
  • Prioritize men's mental health and suicide prevention.
  • Raise awareness and fund research for prostate and testicular cancer.
  • Study environmental toxins, lifestyle factors, and other drivers of poor male health outcomes.
12

Work, Economy and Fairness

  • End discrimination in hiring, promotion, pay and retention.
  • Recognize and value male-dominated essential work.
  • Support entrepreneurship, financial literacy and economic opportunity for men.
  • Reform welfare systems that disincentivize work and fatherhood.
13

National Service and Sacrifice

  • If military service is compulsory, it must apply equally to men and women.
  • Recognize and honor all who serve and protect.
  • Ensure veterans receive the care and respect they deserve.
14

Protection from Legal Abuse

  • Protect men from paternity fraud and reproductive fraud.
  • Mandatory DNA testing at birth when paternity is in question.
  • Reform restraining order laws to prevent misuse.
  • Ensure due process and presumption of innocence for all men.
15

Freedoms and Rights

  • Freedom of speech, thought, association and conscience.
  • Freedom from censorship and ideological coercion.
  • The right to be heard, disagree, and participate in public life.
16

Research, Data and Transparency

  • Collect and publish sex-disaggregated data in all key areas of life.
  • Base policy on evidence, not ideology.
  • Support independent research into issues affecting men.
  • Promote open debate and intellectual honesty.

17 — Our Guiding Philosophy

Rights cannot exist without responsibilities.

Equality cannot exist without reciprocity.

Justice cannot exist with double standards.

Strong men protect society not because they are forced to, but because they choose to.

We build. We provide. We protect. We lead. We take responsibility. We earn respect. We fight for justice. We stand for truth.

18 — Our Commitment

We are committed to peaceful, democratic and lawful advocacy for the rights and well-being of men.

We reject hatred, violence and extremism.

We seek partnerships with anyone who shares our commitment to fairness, truth and justice.

We judge ideas by their merit, not the gender of those who express them.
